# 🟡 Dodoit - The "Stop Forgetting Stuff" Circles

**Dodoit** is a floating note manager for Windows designed specifically for people with the attention span of a goldfish. Inspired by the classic DVD bouncing logo, this app ensures your tasks are never forgotten—mostly because they won't stop hitting the corners of your monitor.

## 🚀 Why do you need this? (Be honest)

Let’s face it: traditional sticky notes are where productivity goes to die. You write "Pay Rent" on a yellow square, stick it to your desktop, and your brain immediately treats it as wallpaper. Two weeks later, you're homeless, but hey—the wallpaper looked nice.

**Dodoit** solves this by weaponizing your own eyes against you. If a task is moving, you can't ignore it. It’s annoying. It’s relentless. It’s the only way you’ll actually do it.

## 🛠️ Features for the Dysfunctional

- **Aggressive Movement:** Notes bounce around your screen like a toddler on espresso. If you don't do the task, at least you can play the "will it hit the corner perfectly?" game.
- **The Aging System (Public Shame):** The longer you ignore a task, the slower it gets. A fresh note is fast and optimistic. A 5-hour-old note moves with the crushing weight of your own procrastination. It’s not "lag"—it’s a physical representation of your guilt.
- **Color Coding:** Use colors to categorize your failures. Red for "Urgent," Blue for "I'll do it later (Lie)," and Yellow for "I forgot why I wrote this."
- **Hover to Pause:** Because we aren't monsters. If you actually decide to work, just hover your mouse over the ball and it will stop moving—much like your career progress.
- **Auto-Persistence:** Even if you restart your PC to "update Windows" (we know you just wanted to close your 50 Chrome tabs), your tasks will be right there waiting for you. There is no escape.

## 📦 Installation (For the Tech-Challenged)

1. Download `dodoit.exe` from the [Releases](https://github.com/AlexandreGuerra/dodoit/releases) tab.
2. If Windows screams about a "Virus" or "Unknown Publisher," just click **"More info"** and **"Run anyway"**. It’s not a virus; it’s just the Windows SmartScreen being a helicopter parent.
3. Throw a shortcut in `shell:startup` so your chores greet you before your coffee does.
